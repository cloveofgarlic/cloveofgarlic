Another thing that I love about this dish is the color. Not only does the color make the dish look pretty, but as a dietitian, I often tell patients to "eat the rainbow." No, I'm not talking about Skittles here, I'm talking about the color of the fruits and vegetables that you eat. The color of the food actually represents what vitamins and minerals are inside! So if you eat a lot of one particular type of vegetable, you are limiting yourself to the amount of vitamins and minerals that are in that one food. Whereas if you have a lot of variety and color, you will likely get all of the nutrients you need in a day. Just look at all that color!

Roasted Vegetable Enchilada
Author: Meagan
Prep time: 30 mins
Cook time: 60 mins
Total time: 1 hour 30 mins
Serves: 4
Ingredients
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into match sticks
  • 1 poblano pepper, cut into match sticks
  • 1 onion, halved and cut into strips
  • 1/2 head of cauliflower, diced
  • 1 sweet potato, diced into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 Tbs olive oil
  • 3 handfuls of spinach (~2oz)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/4 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 1/2 cups salsa (I used a black bean Salsa)
  • 7 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheese
Instructions
  1. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees.
  2. Lightly oil a large baking sheet
  3. Place red bell peppers, poblanos, onion, cauliflower, sweet potato, and corn kernels onto cookie sheet.
  4. Drizzle oil and sprinkle the cumin, chili pepper, and minced garlic over top. Add a generous pinch or two of salt and black pepper, then use your hands to mix ingredients together.
  5. Spread vegetables evenly on baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes until vegetables are tender and begin to brown in spots. Stir or shake the pan every 10 minutes for even roasting.
  6. Remove pan from oven and reduce oven temperature to 350 degrees.
  7. Prepare an 8x8 square baking pan with nonstick spray.
  8. Spread 1/4 cup of salsa into the bottom of the baking pan.
  9. Add a layer of tortilla pieces, to completely cover the salsa.
  10. Top with 1/3 of the vegetables, a handful of spinach, and 1/3 of the cheese. Make a second layer of tortilla, salsa, vegetables, spinach, and cheese.
  11. Top with with a layer of tortillas, salsa, vegetables, and cheese. Cover with aluminum foil.
  12. Bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake another 10 minutes, until cheese is melted and slightly browned.
  13. Let pan sit for 5 minutes then cut into squares. Serve with sour cream and/or a sprinkle of sliced scallions.
