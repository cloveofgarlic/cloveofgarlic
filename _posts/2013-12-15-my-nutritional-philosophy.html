If there is a downside to being an RD it's this: everyone expects you to eat perfectly. When I go home for the holidays, I get a lot of "but you're a dietitian!" when I reach for the desserts... and it drives me nuts!! Why? Well, it all goes back to the foundation of my nutritional philosophy. Moderation.

 

[caption id="attachment_375" align="alignright" width="300"] An RD's best friend, MyPlate.[/caption]

It sounds so simple doesn't it? Clearly, based on the fact that nearly 70% of our population is overweight or obese, it isn't. And it's something even I struggle with during the holidays, or when any chocolate dessert is present. So, here is how moderation can be made easy: Make compromises. It is nice to think of each meal as a MyPlate. But we don't always eat this way. I like to think of the broader picture. If I don't have a vegetable for breakfast, I'll include some snap peas or carrots as a snack. No dairy for lunch? Why not add some non-fat yogurt in that sauce?

And as for dessert, which clearly is not on this plate, don't deny yourself a good treat, but again, adjust for it! Have a smaller slice, not the whole pie. Cut your calories earlier in the day by having a larger salad for lunch- filled with lots of fresh fruits and veggies and a nice fat-free dressing.  And enjoy your treat! If you swallow it in 1 bite you are likely to want to go back for more. Take at least 10 bites before you swallow and really take in that flavor!

So, don't be shocked when a dessert recipe of mine calls for 2 sticks of butter. And please, don't yell at me for it. No one, despite what they tell you, is a perfect eater. And sometimes applesauce just cannot replace butter...I'm sorry. I will try to make  suggestions for great nutritious sides to serve with my carb loaded but oh-so-scrumptious entree, but I leave it to you to make compromises and practice moderation with what you eat!
