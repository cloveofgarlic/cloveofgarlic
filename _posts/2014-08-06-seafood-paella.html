Seafood paella is one of the recipes I always make when friends/family come to visit, or for special occasions, or just for a special date night... any excuse we can find, we make it. Right now, it is probably my favorite recipe, even trumping my dad's chicken parm, which is really saying something.

 

Paella is a traditional Spanish dish of short-grain rice, saffron and good quality olive oil. The 3 most popular versions are seafood, vegetarian, or mixed meat and seafood. I love the seafood and mixed meat/seafood. I am posting the mixed because I find the seafood balances the spice of the chorizo and gives it a subtle rich flavor. If you aren't a sausage or spice fan, just omit it.

 

Note: You do not need to have a paella pan to make this recipe- for over a year I used a large, 2 inch deep skillet, and it turned out great each time. The paella pan just allows the bottom to crisp more evenly- and Dan loves the charred bottom.

Fun facts about paella:

  • The beginning mix of onion, garlic, parsley, and olive oil is called a soffritto. It smells like heaven.
  • Chorizo is a spanish sausage and can be found near the Italian sausage. If your grocery store doesn't have it, you can use hot Italian sausage as a substitute (though it is a bit spicier the Chorizo)
  • The toasted rice bottom is called socarrat.
  • If you can't find Spanish short grain rice- you can use Arborio rice (an Italian short grain, used for risotto)
  • Saffron is the most expensive spice in the world at $500-$5,000 dollars per pound. It is what provides the paella's yellow/orange coloring.

Seafood Paella
Recipe Type: Entree
Cuisine: Spanish
Author: Meagan
Prep time: 15 mins
Cook time: 50 mins
Total time: 1 hour 5 mins
Serves: 4-6
Ingredients
  • 3 Tbs extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 Spanish chorizo sausages, thickly sliced
  • 1 Medium onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 bunch flat-leaf parsley leaves, chopped, reserve some for garnish
  • 2 Large tomatoes, diced
  • 2 cups short grain Spanish rice
  • 4 cups fat free, low sodium chicken broth, warmed
  • Generous pinch saffron threads
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 dozen littleneck clams or mussels, scrubbed
  • 8 jumbo shrimp, peeled and de-veined
  • 8 Largo scallops
  • 1/2 Red pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup sweet peas, frozen and thawed
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
Instructions
  1. Heat oil in a paella pan over medium-high heat. Saute the chorizo until browned, remove and reserve.
  2. In the same pan, make a sofrito by sauteing the onions, garlic, and parsley. Cook for 2 or 3 minutes on a medium heat.
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add diced tomatoes and cook until the mixture caramelizes a bit and the flavors meld- about 10 minutes.
  4. Fold in the rice and stir-fry to coat the grains, cook 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour in chicken broth and simmer for 15 minutes, gently stirring so the rice cooks evenly and absorbs 2/3 of the liquid.
  6. Add chorizo, saffron and salt. Cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Give the paella a good shake and let it simmer, without stirring, until the rice is al dente- about 15 minutes. During the last 10 minutes of cooking, when the rice is filling the pan, add the shrimp and scallops.
  8. With 5 minutes remaining, add the mussels, peppers, and peas. Cover pan.
  9. Uncover pan and test rice. Most to all liquid should be absorbed. Test temperature of scallops- they should be at 145 degrees F or higher.
  10. Turn heat on high for 45 seconds to allow bottom to toast.
  11. Let sit for 5 minutes then serve with parsley garnish and lemon.
