Now that I'm done making excuses for my bad behavior, I would like to present one of my FAVORITE party recipes: The Garlic Cream Cheese Log. This log pretty much destroys any traditional dip out there. It is scrumptious on crackers, veggies, and pretty much anything you set your mind to.

      

To give credit where credit is due, this recipe is my Mom's. I pretty much force her to make it for all of our family gatherings that require an appetizer. Her trick to the amazing garlic flavor? Letting it sit in the fridge overnight before adding the parsley. You certainly can make this day-of, but try to let the rolled log sit as long as possible. Think of it as a fine red wine...or just drink the wine with the spread...now there's an idea!

I highly recommend using plastic wrap to create the log shape. Simply plop the cream cheese mixture in the center of the wrap, fold the wrap around the blob and roll into the log shape. When you are ready to add the parsley, unwrap, sprinkle the parsley, and roll the log around while still on the wrap. It will save your hands from a sticky mess!!

    

 

 

Garlic Cream Cheese Log
Recipe Type: Appetizer
Author: Meagan DiVito
Prep time: 15 mins
Cook time: 5 mins
Total time: 20 mins
Serves: 8
Ingredients
  • 8 oz low fat cream cheese, softened
  • 2 tsp garlic, minced and toasted
  • 1/4 cup red onion
  • 1/8 tsp black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp fresh parsley, minced
Instructions
  1. Toast garlic in a nonstick pan on low heat until slightly browned.
  2. Add garlic, red onion, and black pepper to cream cheese. Blend with hand mixer.
  3. Place cream cheese mixture onto a 12 inch long piece of plastic wrap. Fold the wrap width-wise around the cream cheese. Roll the mixture to create a log. Twist the ends of the plastic wrap to create a tight seal. Refrigerate overnight.
  4. Unwrap log and sprinkle parsley over log.
  5. Serve with vegetables and crackers.
