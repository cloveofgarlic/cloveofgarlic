I LOVE breakfast. It is my favorite meal of the day, and without it I wouldn't be able to function in the morning. One of my most memorable special-occasion dishes is my grandmother's blueberry french toast bake. She would make this at her restaurant and for us kids as a Christmas morning treat every year. It is very holiday appropriate, as it is filled with cinnamon, nutmeg and allspice, but you can substitute the blueberries for other fruits like raspberries, strawberries, or cherries!

While fresh is best, you can use frozen fruit for this recipe. This time around, I used fresh blueberries for the bake and frozen for the sauce. If only berries weren't a seasonal commodity...

    

 The secret of this recipe is letting it sit in the egg wash overnight. It allows all of the egg to soak through and makes a super fluffy dish. It also makes the morning prep easy, as you just throw it from the fridge directly into the oven!!

      

     

During the last 15 minutes of baking, make the blueberry sauce. While my grandmother just dumps the whole pan of sauce on top of the bake, I prefer to put it on the side. Not only does this allow you to control how much extra sugar you add, but it allows for preserving leftovers. You can also use any extra sauce during the week by adding it to oatmeal or yogurt!

    

Recipes are all about personal preference, so please modify accordingly. I always taste things as I cook and make adjustments. I prefer lots of spices, so I added extra cinnamon and nutmeg to my sauce that isn't reflected in this recipe.

Blueberry French Toast Bake
Recipe Type: Breakfast
Author: Meagan DiVito
Prep time: 10 mins
Cook time: 50 mins
Total time: 1 hour
Serves: 6
Ingredients
  • 12 slices whole wheat bread, cut into 1" x 1" cubes
  • 10 eggs
  • 1 cup 1% or skim milk
  • 2 cups blueberries, fresh or frozen
  • 8 oz low fat cream cheese
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp allspice
Instructions
  1. 1. Grease a 9" x 13" baking dish and set aside.
  2. 2. Beat together eggs, milk, vanilla, and spices in a large bowl.
  3. 3. Add cubed whole wheat bread to egg and spice mixture, toss until most of the egg is absorbed.
  4. 4. Place 1/3 of the bread/egg mixture onto the greased pan. Sprinkle 3/4 cup of blueberries on top of the bread. Slice 1/3 of the cream cheese into 1tsp to 1 Tbs sized pieces and place onto the bake.
  5. 5. Repeat previous step until all of the ingredients are layered. You should have 3 layers total.
  6. 6. Wrap bake with tinfoil and place in refrigerator overnight.
  7. [br]
  8. 7. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  9. 8. Once preheated, place pan in oven and cook wrapped for 30 minutes.
  10. 9. Remove tinfoil and bake for another 20 minutes or until slightly browned with a firm center.
  11. 10. Sit for 5-10 minutes prior to serving.

Blueberry Sauce
Recipe Type: Breakfast
Author: Meagan DiVito
Prep time: 2 mins
Cook time: 15 mins
Total time: 17 minutes
Serves: 6
Ingredients
  • 1 cup water
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 Tbs corn starch
  • 1 cup blueberries, fresh or frozen
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
Instructions
  1. 1. Combine water, sugar, and cornstarch in a small sauce pan. Bring to a boil and stir until thickened. About 10 minutes.
  2. 2. Reduce heat and add blueberries and spices to thickened sauce. Simmer for additional 5 minutes or until most of the berries burst.
  3. 3. Pour desired amount over Blueberry French Toast bake and Enjoy!
