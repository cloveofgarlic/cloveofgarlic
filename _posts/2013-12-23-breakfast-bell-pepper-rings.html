Happy Holidays, everyone! Dan and I have been absolutely swamped this week with flying back to New England for Christmas. We have had lots of family time, an engagement party, engagement photos, looking at some venues, and 2 separate Christmases with our families. So, needless to say, not much cooking has been happening. But don't fear friends! I discovered an incredibly easy and stunning breakfast recipe for my family this morning (which uses some Christmas dinner leftovers no less)!!

The recipe is simple: take a pepper and slice it into 1" thick rings and fill them with whatever egg/breakfast amazingness you want! While over-easy is probably the easiest, Dan and I prefer scrambled eggs. So, we did some both ways with ham, onion, and cheese added to the scrambled peppers. SUPER easy! I paired this with some simple homefries and voila! Delicious breakfast.

  

I recommend starting the potatoes 15 minutes or so before the peppers. While those are cooking, prep the eggs and any fun ingredients you are going to put in them!! Some filling suggestions are tomatoes, spinach, onion, cheese, and sort of breakfast meat (we had leftover Christmas ham). You could also be super fancy and add some fresh basil or chives for a great fresh feel. Have fun with all the possibilities!

 

  

 

Homie Fries
Recipe Type: Breakfast
Author: Meagan DiVito
Prep time: 10 mins
Cook time: 30 mins
Total time: 40 mins
Serves: 4
Ingredients
  • 4 Medium Potatoes, cut into 1"x1/2" chunks
  • 1/2 Medium Onion, diced
  • 2 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • 3 Tbs EVOO
  • 1/2 tsp Paprika
  • 1 tsp Rosemary
  • 1 tsp Thyme
  • 1 tsp Savory Seasoning
Instructions
  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Wash and cut the potatoes, onion, and garlic. Add to heated pan.
  3. Add spices and sauté in pan with lid on for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove lid and cook for an additional 15 minutes until the potatoes are soft and browned on both sides.

Breakfast Bell Pepper Rings
Recipe Type: Breakfast
Author: Meagan DiVito
Prep time: 10 mins
Cook time: 20 mins
Total time: 30 mins
Serves: 4
This recipe was inspired by: http://stovetopstories.wordpress.com/2012/01/19/red-pepper-and-egg-breakfast/
Ingredients
  • 2 Bell Peppers, cut into 1/2-1" rings
  • 6 Eggs
  • 3 Tbs EVOO
  • Rosemary
  • Thyme
  • [br]
  • [b]If Scrambling Eggs:[/b]
  • 1/4 cup Skim Milk
  • 1/3 cup Onion, diced
  • 1/3 cup Ham, diced
  • 1/4 cup Mozzarella cheese, shredded
Instructions
  1. Wash and cut the bell peppers into 1/2" to 1" rings, making sure to keep the rings as even as possible. Size of rings will depend on type of eggs being cooked and any additional fillings.
  2. Heat EVOO in a flat skillet over medium heat. Place bell peppers onto skillet and cook for 2 minutes on each side. Increase time to 3 minutes if using 1" thick ring.
  3. Place egg into pepper ring. If egg leaks below pepper ring, use spatula to hold ring firmly against the pan until the egg sets.
  4. If using scrambled eggs, add a tablespoon of onion and ham, mix well using a spoon until egg is cooked through- about 3 to 5 minutes depending on how full the pepper is. Once cooked, sprinkle 1 Tbs cheese over top of egg.
  5. Sprinkle Rosemary, Thyme, on top of over-easy eggs
